Lola Project: William


Lola's main archetype is the Rebel. As a woman living in a patriarchal, drug-dominated society, she defiantly rejects the strict social norms by asserting herself as the true leader of her gang.

As the leader, society demands she maintain a ruthless public image to command respect. Yet her caregiver instincts are strengthened by forming protective relationships that society does not typically value.
For example, when Hector disobeys during a mission, Lola must balance the dual roles society has placed her in - as a leader enforcing order versus a caregiver wanting his welfare. She ultimately chooses a response befitting both roles.

Over time, Lola develops a deeper commitment to caring for those marginalized by society, like Lucy. However, her social circumstances as a criminal woman limited by the oppressive drug world trap her in the role of gang leader through necessity.
